Understanding Peer Pressure

Direct Peer Pressure

Direct peer pressure involves straightforward demands or requests to engage in specific behaviors.

  • Being persuaded to try substances
  • Encouragement to skip classes

Indirect Peer Pressure

Indirect peer pressure can be subtle and involves perceived social expectations.

  • Feeling the need to conform to fashion trends
  • Pressure to achieve academically

Strategies to Combat Peer Pressure

1. Build Confidence

Strengthen your self-esteem by engaging in activities that make you feel good about yourself.

2. Choose Friends Wisely

Surround yourself with supportive friends who respect your decisions.

3. Practice Saying No

Learn to say no confidently and practice different ways to refuse peer pressure.
